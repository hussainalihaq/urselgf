Our Roots / Story / Legacy

A Century of Refining
Nature's Gold

Our story began with Malik Ameer Bakhsh Khokhar, a revolutionary farmer who founded the Ameer Bakhsh Farm in 1966. His spirit and dedication transformed our family lands into a sanctuary for the world's finest mangoes.

By moving beyond early or late varieties to curate only peak-season fruit, we ensure a true farm-to-table experience with no middleman. We aren't just selling a product; we want you to taste a part of our heritage, legacy, and culture.

Each variety is a distinct chapter in our heritage. Hand-selected, individually graded, and presented with the reverence they deserve.

The Honeyed Giant

Sindhri

Large, visually striking, and intensely aromatic. The Sindhri offers a perfect balance of tartness and honeyed sweetness, with a firm texture ideal for slicing. Prized by chefs and connoisseurs worldwide.

The Crown Jewel

Anwar Ratol

Small in stature, immense in character. The Anwar Ratol is celebrated for its intense, floral aroma and remarkably sweet, fiberless flesh. A fleeting luxury of the mid-summer harvest — available for only 3 weeks each season.

scaleWeight: 80–120g per fruit
calendar_monthSeason: July (limited window)
starBrix: 22–26° sweetness index

The Nectar

Chaunsa

Renowned for its exceptionally rich, syrupy sweetness and minimal fiber. The Chaunsa is a late-season delicacy that offers a profound, melting texture — the connoisseur's favorite among Pakistani mangoes.
