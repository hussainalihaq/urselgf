Himalayan Pink Salt

250 million years of geological perfection. Sourced from the legendary Khewra Salt Mines in Pakistan — the world's second-largest salt mine.

The Origin Story

The Khewra Salt Mines

Discovered by Alexander the Great's army in 326 BC, the Khewra mines sit at the foothills of the Salt Range in Punjab, Pakistan. They produce over 350,000 tons of salt annually from deposits formed during the Precambrian era.

326 BC

Discovery by Alexander's Army

Horses of Alexander's cavalry began licking the rocks, leading soldiers to discover the mineral-rich salt deposits in the foothills of the Salt Range.

1872

Modern Mining Established

British engineers introduced systematic mining techniques, establishing the mine as one of the most significant mineral extraction sites in British India.

Today

Global Wellness Icon

Himalayan pink salt has become a cornerstone of wellness, culinary, and interior design globally — exported to over 100 countries for food, spa, and décor use.
